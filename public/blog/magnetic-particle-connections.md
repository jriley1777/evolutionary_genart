# Magnetic Particle Connections

Magnetic field system with attractors, repellers, and electromagnetic-style particle interactions.

## Concept

- Particles behave like charges in a magnetic field: attractors pull them in, repellers push them away.
- Connections or lines can form between nearby particles, suggesting a network.
- Movement is driven by field forces plus optional flow or noise.
- Visual style is clean and diagram-like.

## Technical notes

- Attractors and repellers are point forces with falloff by distance.
- Connection logic uses a distance threshold between particle pairs.
- Colors and line weights can reflect strength or type of interaction.
